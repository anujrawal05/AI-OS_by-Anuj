Structured Academy Curriculum

Learn core unit economics, customer pipelines, and leverage options. Designed to be accessible for MBA graduates and beginners alike to understand business from first principles.

Module 01 // Introduction

What is Business?

▼
Core Principle

A business is a structured system that creates, delivers, and captures value. At its simplest, it is a machine that solves a problem for a group of people and charges a fee for the solution.

Case Example

An individual buys raw cotton for ₹100, spins it into a premium custom canvas bag, and sells it to local shoppers for ₹500. By adding value (labor, utility, design), they capture ₹400 in gross returns.

Key Checklist
  • Identify the core value transaction before launching.
  • Draft the basic product/service delivery structure.
  • Analyze the cost structure of raw production versus sales price.
Module 02 // Strategy

Why Businesses Exist

▲
Core Principle

Businesses exist to resolve inefficiencies in the market. If everyone could build everything themselves, trade would not exist. A company operates to pool specialized resources and solve complex customer objectives efficiently.

Case Example

Instead of each homeowner digging their own well, a water-utility business builds a central reservoir system and piping layout, charging a fraction of individual costs while yielding a high total margin.

Key Checklist
  • Determine what specific market friction you are eliminating.
  • Verify if your solution is cheaper/better than building it in-house.
Module 03 // Finance

How Companies Make Money

▲
Core Principle

Revenue models define how value translates to cash flow. Companies make money by pricing their products based on cost-plus margins, subscription value-delivery, or premium outcome-based structures.

Case Example

SaaS apps charge ₹99/month for access to dynamic prompts. Because the variable cost of serving each user is nearly ₹0, the margins scale extremely fast with volume.

Key Checklist
  • Select a clean pricing model: Transactional, Subscription, or Service Commission.
  • Confirm that pricing exceeds variable cost-of-goods-sold (COGS).
Module 04 // Architecture

Business Models

▲
Core Principle

A business model is the blueprint of how a company creates value. Common models include E-Commerce (selling goods), SaaS (software subscriptions), Agency (custom services), and Marketplaces (connecting buyers and sellers).

Case Example

An AI Agency builds automated tools for realtors and charges a ₹30,000 setup fee + ₹5,000/month recurring maintenance, building high recurring value.

Key Checklist
  • Select the model matching your initial skill levels and resource availability.
  • Ensure the business model supports low operating expenses (OPEX).
Module 05 // Discovery

Finding Profitable Problems

▲
Core Principle

Profitable problems are painful, frequent, and expensive. If customers are already spending money trying to solve a friction point (even manually), it represents a high-potential opportunity.

Case Example

Local doctors spending 3 hours daily writing patient summary logs manually are willing to pay ₹10,000/month for an automated transcription tool.

Key Checklist
  • Validate that the target audience is already paying to solve this problem.
  • Interview 5 prospects about their daily administrative bottlenecks.
Module 06 // Distribution

Finding Customers

▲
Core Principle

Customer acquisition channels include organic content, cold outreach (email, social DMs), and paid advertising. Finding customers requires matching your pitch with where they congregate online.

Case Example

A design agency uses automated LinkedIn scripts to contact 50 marketing managers daily, pitching custom visual assets and capturing 3 new high-value accounts monthly.

Key Checklist
  • Identify the top community hubs (Reddit, LinkedIn, Twitter) for your niche.
  • Draft a 3-step cold message demonstrating clear value up-front.
Module 07 // Identity

Brand Building

▲
Core Principle

A brand is the perception customers hold of your company. It is established through design consistency, clear marketing copy, visual asset quality, and reliable service delivery.

Case Example

By using clean monochrome assets, glassmorphism layouts, and highly readable fonts, a boutique agency immediately charges double the pricing of competitors.

Key Checklist
  • Define your brand's core color values and typography systems.
  • Build a professional portfolio website to validate your offers.
Module 08 // Psychology

Sales Psychology

▲
Core Principle

Sales is value demonstration, not negotiation. Focus on selling outcomes (e.g. 50 new qualified leads per week) instead of features (e.g. custom database setup). Align your pricing to the ROI they receive.

Case Example

An automation consultant charges ₹50,000 for a pipeline by proving it saves the company ₹1,50,000 in monthly administrative staffing costs.

Key Checklist
  • Refocus your landing page copy around the outcome the buyer experiences.
  • Incorporate social proof, case studies, or interactive demos.
Module 09 // Monetization

Digital Business

▲
Core Principle

Digital businesses operate globally with minimal inventory constraints. Selling e-books, online courses, software, or digital services lets you serve infinite clients without manufacturing overhead.

Case Example

A creator publishes a database of business templates. Once created, they duplicate and sell it 1,000 times globally, achieving ₹0 marginal distribution costs.

Key Checklist
  • Select a digital delivery system (Gumroad, Razorpay, Stripe link).
  • Build a clean asset checkout flow that fires instantly upon payment receipt.
Module 10 // Integration

AI in Business

▲
Core Principle

AI is the ultimate force multiplier. Instead of hiring copywriters, analysts, and developers, you can use generative models and automation tools to perform these tasks in seconds, keeping operations lean.

Case Example

A single entrepreneur uses AI coding agents to build, test, and host a SaaS product in 4 hours, doing the work that used to require a development team.

Key Checklist
  • Map out administrative tasks you currently execute manually.
  • Write targeted system instructions to execute these tasks via API.
Module 11 // Operations

Automation

▲
Core Principle

Automation connects fragmented apps together. Using webhooks and workflow triggers (like Make, Zapier), you can sync user signup events to email lists, create invoices, and alert team rooms automatically.

Case Example

An automation setup detects new client emails, parses the text via LLM to extract requirements, matches a contract template, and emails a quote in under 3 minutes.

Key Checklist
  • Diagram the workflow structure of your customer intake data.
  • Link your lead capture forms directly to CRM databases using automated flows.
Module 12 // Expansion

Scaling Companies

▲
Core Principle

Scaling is shifting focus from delivery to distribution. Once your unit economics are positive and operations are automated, focus on scaling marketing spend and hiring leadership to run daily execution.

Case Example

A service business delegates client support to automated agents and contracts out production, spending their entire weekly time optimizing acquisition conversions.

Key Checklist
  • Ensure the business can operate without your manual daily intervention.
  • Diversify distribution channels to avoid relying on a single traffic source.
