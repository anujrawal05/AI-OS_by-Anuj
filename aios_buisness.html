Module 01 // Introduction

What is Business?

▼
Core Principle
  • A business is a structured system that creates, delivers, and captures value.
  • At its core, it is a machine that solves a customer's problem.
  • It charges a fee greater than the total cost of delivery to yield profit.
Real business example
  • Canvas Bag Merchant: Buys canvas for ₹100, pays tailor ₹150, sells bag for ₹500. COGS is ₹250, capturing ₹250 in gross returns for creating a beautiful, functional accessory.
Common Beginner Mistake
  • Assuming revenue is profit: Spending money before accounting for overhead costs.
  • Neglecting expenses like payment fees, hosting, and marketing.
AI Implementation Opportunity
  • Use AI spreadsheets (Claude/ChatGPT + Sheets) to forecast cash flows.
  • Model pricing margins and cost variations in seconds.
Practical Task
  • List 3 products or services you purchased this month and write down their estimated cost to build versus the price you paid.
Module 02 // Architecture

Business Models

▼
Core Principle

A business model describes how your company makes money. Common models include Software-as-a-Service (SaaS), Services Agencies, E-Commerce, and Marketplaces.

Model Comparison Matrix
Model Margin Setup Time Example
SaaS High (80%+) Months Netflix, Notion
Agency Medium (50%+) Days Consultants, Dev Shops
E-Comm Low (15-30%) Weeks Shopify stores
Real business example

Stripe: Instead of selling software, Stripe takes a 2-3% cut of every transaction. They succeed because they scale in proportion to their customer's revenue growth.

Common Beginner Mistake

Building a SaaS from scratch when a simple service agency could start bringing in cash in 48 hours.

Module 03 // Discovery

Finding Profitable Problems

▼
Core Principle

Profitable problems are painful, frequent, and expensive. If customers are already spending time or money to solve it manually, they will pay you to solve it cleanly.

Real business example

Programmatic SEO for Clinics: Local clinics spend ₹30,000/month hiring writers to write blogs manually. An agency that generates 50 localized SEO landing pages using structured AI data saves the clinic time and money.

Common Beginner Mistake

Building a product first and trying to find a market later. Always find a starving crowd first.

AI Implementation Opportunity

Use AI web scrapers to parse Reddit and community directories for common administrative bottlenecks.

📝 Concept Check-Quiz

Before writing any code or buying inventory, what is the best way to validate a problem?

Module 04 // Psychology

Customer Psychology

▼
Core Principle
  • People do not buy features; they buy transformative outcomes.
  • They purchase products to move from a state of pain to ease, status, or efficiency.
  • Always align your marketing and offer headlines with their underlying desires.
Real business example
  • Apple iCloud: Apple does not sell "secure digital servers" (features). They sell "never losing your family photos" (peace of mind, emotional benefit).
Common Beginner Mistake
  • Listing technical jargon (e.g., 'Node.js, MongoDB') instead of the outcome.
  • Forgetting to explain the outcome (e.g., 'Saves 4 hours of work daily').
Practical Task
  • Take any product you use and rewrite its headline: 1 version focusing on features, and 1 version focusing on emotional outcome.
Module 05 // Monetization

Pricing Strategies

▼
Core Principle

Pricing dictates your positioning. Cost-plus pricing covers costs, but value-based pricing charges based on the exact financial outcome or savings you deliver to the buyer.

Pricing Models Comparison
Model How It Works Client Perception
Cost-Plus Cost of work + 20% margin Friction (tries to lower hourly rate)
Value-Based Charge 10-30% of total ROI delivered High trust (win-win alignment)
Real business example

Consulting Agency: An AI developer builds a chatbot that replaces a ₹1,20,000/month support agent. Instead of charging ₹5,000 (hourly rate), they charge ₹40,000 flat setup. The customer gladly pays because they save ₹80,000 in month one.

📝 Concept Check-Quiz

Which pricing strategy allows a business to charge high margins aligned with client outcomes?

Module 06 // Lead Gen

Marketing Basics

▼
Core Principle

Marketing is building attention. Inbound marketing pulls customers using helpful resources and templates; outbound marketing directly reaches out to prospective users via emails or calls.

Real business example

Notion Templates: Notion built a massive organic pipeline by letting creators build and share templates. Users distribute templates organically, bringing millions of free signups to Notion.

Common Beginner Mistake

Spending money on Facebook or Google ads to promote an unvalidated product. Organize organic content loops first.

Module 07 // Conversions

Sales & Pitching

▼
Core Principle

Sales is collaborative problem-solving, not high-pressure pitching. The goal is to ask strategic questions, outline the financial impact of their current issues, and introduce your offer as the direct fix.

Real business example

Consultative Discovery: An automation consultant starts a call asking: 'What administrative task is costing you the most hours this week?' Once identified, they draft a visual roadmap showing how AI handles it automatically.

Common Beginner Mistake

Talking 90% of the time during a call. Always let the prospect explain their operational pain first.

📝 Concept Check-Quiz

What is the primary objective of a consultative sales call?

Module 08 // Operations

Brand Building

▼
Core Principle

A brand is a promise of quality. It is built through design consistency, clear marketing copy, high-fidelity visual assets, and delivering on your promises.

Real business example

Linear App: Linear maintains a premium, dark-themed, ultra-fast website layout. Because their product aesthetics look and feel premium, they charge higher rates than competitor ticketing apps.

Common Beginner Mistake

Using random colors, low-resolution images, and messy font sizes that make your platform look unprofessional.

Practical Task
  • Pick a consistent 2-font typography system and a 3-color palette for your landing page assets.
Module 09 // Systems

Operational Automation

▼
Core Principle
  • Automation connects separate applications using webhooks and database triggers.
  • It executes repetitive operations automatically to eliminate manual oversight.
  • Integrations like Make and Zapier save hours of administration tasks.
Real business example
  • Automated Onboarding: Client checkout triggers automation that automatically creates folder structures, drafts a contract PDF, and emails custom onboarding steps.
Common Beginner Mistake
  • Automating a broken process: Always verify a process works manually first.
  • Adding scripts before securing initial customers.

Using AI in Business

▼
Core Principle
  • Artificial Intelligence acts as the ultimate leverage multiplier.
  • Single-operator startups can run copywriting, code generation, and voice synthesis.
  • Enables a solo builder to execute tasks that historically required a full operations team.
Real business example
  • Midjourney: A design studio with a team of under 15 people serves millions of active paying subscribers by utilizing AI image generators.

Module 11 // Growth

Scaling Companies

▼
Core Principle

Scaling is shifting focus from delivery to distribution. Once your unit economics are positive and operations are automated, focus on scaling marketing spend and hiring leadership to run daily execution.

Real business example

Drop-Servicing Agency: A company contracts out raw software delivery to vetted freelancers. The founder focuses 100% of their time on marketing, closing ₹20,00,000 in monthly sales while taking a 40% clean cut.

Common Beginner Mistake

Trying to scale a business that has negative margins. Fix unit economics before expanding traffic.

Module 12 // Leverage

Hiring Contractors

▼
Core Principle

Hiring frees up your time to focus on growth. Start by outsourcing low-leverage tasks (like administrative entries) to specialized contractors before hiring full-time staff.

Real business example

Boutique Studio: A design studio founder hires 2 skilled freelance visual designers on Upwork at ₹1,500/hour while charging clients a project rate equivalent to ₹4,000/hour, building an arbitrage margin.

Common Beginner Mistake

Hiring full-time employees with monthly salary overheads before securing consistent monthly incoming cash flow.

Module 13 // Finance

Financial Basics

▼
Core Principle

Every business must track its Income Statement. The most important metrics are Revenue, Burn Rate (monthly costs), and net cash flow (cash entering minus cash leaving).

Real business example

Unit Profitability: A consultant tracks their monthly software overhead (₹4,000) and hosting (₹1,000). By generating ₹1,50,000/month from a single client retainership, they operate with a 96% net margin.

Module 14 // Regulatory

Legal Basics & Contracts

▼
Core Principle
  • Contracts protect your work quality and guarantee transaction payments.
  • Setup a formal entity (LLC or Pvt Ltd) as you scale to limit legal liability.
  • Keep business and personal assets strictly separated to shield your savings.
Entity Comparison
Entity Liability Setup Effort
Sole Prop Unlimited (Personal risk) Zero
LLC / Pvt Ltd Limited to business assets Moderate (Requires registration)

Module 15 // Execution

Launching Your First Business

▼
Core Principle
  • The single goal of launching is to gather feedback and learn.
  • Ship a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) in under 24 hours.
  • Put your offer in front of real customers and iterate based on their usage behavior.
Real business example
  • Dropbox MVP: Dropbox launched with a simple 3-minute video showing the product working, capturing 75,000 signups overnight before writing a single line of backend code.
Common Beginner Mistake
  • Delaying your launch for months to refine a product that nobody has validated they want.
  • Launch fast and adapt based on actual usage.
Launch Day Checklist
  • Configure a single-page landing site showing a clean value pitch.
  • Incorporate a payment checkout link (Razorpay/Stripe).
  • Directly share the page with 10 prospective target users.
